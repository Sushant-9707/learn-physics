    Current Electricity

    Current electricity deals with the flow of electric charges through conductors. It explains how electrical energy is transferred and used in practical systems.

    Core Ideas

    Electric Current

    Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor.

    It requires a closed circuit and a potential difference.

    Drift Velocity

    Drift velocity is the average velocity with which charge carriers move in a conductor.

    It is very small, even though electric signals travel quickly.

    Ohm’s Law

    Ohm’s law states that current is directly proportional to voltage for a conductor at constant temperature.

    It defines the concept of electrical resistance.

    Resistance

    Resistance opposes the flow of electric current.

    It depends on material, length, cross-sectional area, and temperature.

    EMF and Cells

    Electromotive force represents the energy supplied per unit charge by a source.

    Cells convert chemical energy into electrical energy.

    Kirchhoff’s Laws

    Kirchhoff’s laws apply conservation of charge and energy to electrical circuits.

    They allow analysis of complex multi-loop circuits.

    Electric Power

    Electric power measures the rate at which electrical energy is consumed or transferred.

    It explains energy usage in electrical devices.
