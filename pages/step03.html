    Motion in One Dimension

    Motion in one dimension describes movement along a straight line. It forms the foundation of kinematics and introduces how position, velocity, and acceleration evolve with time.

    Core Ideas

    Position & Displacement

    Position describes the location of an object relative to a chosen origin. Displacement is the change in position and includes both magnitude and direction.

    Displacement depends only on initial and final positions, not on the path taken.

    Speed & Velocity

    Speed is a scalar quantity that measures how fast an object moves. Velocity is a vector quantity that includes both speed and direction.

    In one-dimensional motion, direction is often represented by a sign such as positive or negative.

    Acceleration

    Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with time. It can occur due to changes in speed, direction, or both.

    Uniform acceleration means the acceleration remains constant over time.

    Equations of Motion

    For motion with constant acceleration, kinematic equations relate displacement, velocity, acceleration, and time.

    These equations allow prediction of motion without knowing the entire path.
