    Electrostatics

    Electrostatics studies electric charges at rest and the forces they exert. It forms the foundation for understanding electricity, circuits, and electromagnetic fields.

    Core Ideas

    Electric Charge

    Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter responsible for electrical interactions. Charges can be positive or negative, and like charges repel while unlike charges attract.

    Charge is conserved and quantized, meaning it exists in discrete units.

    Coulomb’s Law

    Coulomb’s law describes the force between two stationary point charges. The force depends on the product of charges and inversely on the square of distance.

    It shows how electric force weakens with increasing separation.

    Electric Field

    The electric field represents the region around a charge where it exerts force on other charges.

    It allows electric interactions to be described without direct contact.

    Electric Field Lines

    Electric field lines visualize the direction and strength of an electric field.

    The density of lines indicates field strength, and lines never intersect.

    Electric Dipole

    An electric dipole consists of two equal and opposite charges separated by a small distance.

    Dipoles experience torque in external electric fields.

    Gauss’s Law

    Gauss’s law relates electric flux through a closed surface to the charge enclosed.

    It simplifies field calculations for highly symmetric charge distributions.
