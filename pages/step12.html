    Rotational Dynamics

    Rotational dynamics explains why objects start rotating, slow down, or change their rotational motion. It connects forces to rotation through torque and angular momentum.

    Core Ideas

    Torque

    Torque is the rotational equivalent of force. It determines how effectively a force can rotate an object about an axis.

    Greater torque produces greater angular acceleration.

    Moment of Inertia

    Moment of inertia measures how resistant an object is to changes in rotational motion.

    It depends on both the mass of the object and how that mass is distributed relative to the axis.

    Newton’s Laws for Rotation

    Newton’s laws apply to rotational motion when force is replaced by torque and mass is replaced by moment of inertia.

    These laws explain why heavier or wider objects are harder to rotate.

    Angular Momentum

    Angular momentum describes the rotational motion of a system.

    In the absence of external torque, angular momentum remains conserved, explaining phenomena like a spinning skater speeding up when pulling in their arms.
