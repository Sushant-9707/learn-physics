    Center of Mass

    The center of mass is the point that represents the average position of mass in a system. It allows complex systems of particles to be analyzed as if all their mass were concentrated at a single point.

    Core Ideas

    Definition of Center of Mass

    The center of mass is a point that moves as if the entire mass of the system were concentrated there and all external forces acted at that point.

    For symmetrical objects with uniform mass, the center of mass lies at the geometric center.

    Center of Mass of a System of Particles

    For multiple particles, the center of mass depends on both the masses and positions of the particles.

    Heavier particles influence the center of mass more strongly than lighter ones.

    Motion of the Center of Mass

    The motion of the center of mass depends only on the external forces acting on the system.

    Internal forces cancel out, meaning explosions or collisions inside a system do not affect the overall motion of the center of mass.

    Applications of Center of Mass

    The concept is widely used in mechanics, from analyzing the motion of projectiles to understanding the stability of objects.

    It explains why athletes adjust their body position to maintain balance and why vehicles are designed with low centers of mass.
