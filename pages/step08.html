    Conservation of Energy

    Energy does not magically appear or vanish. It changes form. The principle of conservation of energy states that the total energy of an isolated system remains constant.

    Core Ideas

    Law of Conservation of Energy

    The total energy of an isolated system remains constant over time. Energy can change form, but it cannot be created or destroyed.

    This principle applies to all physical processes, from falling objects to nuclear reactions.

    Mechanical Energy

    Mechanical energy is the sum of kinetic energy and potential energy. In systems where only conservative forces act, mechanical energy remains constant.

    This makes energy-based analysis simpler than force-based methods.

    Non-Conservative Forces

    Forces like friction and air resistance are non-conservative. They convert mechanical energy into other forms such as heat or sound.

    Even then, total energy is still conserved when all forms are considered.

    Mass–Energy Equivalence

    Energy and mass are not separate concepts. Mass itself is a form of energy. This relationship is described by the famous equation E = mc².

    In this view, matter is simply energy that has taken the form of mass. This idea explains how enormous amounts of energy can be released from small amounts of matter.
