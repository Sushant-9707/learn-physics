    Oscillations

    Oscillatory motion occurs when a system moves repeatedly back and forth about a stable equilibrium position. This type of motion is fundamental to physics and appears in mechanical systems, electrical circuits, atomic vibrations, and even biological processes.

    Core Ideas

    Periodic Motion

    Motion is said to be periodic if it repeats itself after equal intervals of time. Examples include the motion of a pendulum, vibrations of a guitar string, and alternating current in electrical circuits.

    Periodic motion provides the foundation for understanding oscillations and wave phenomena.

    Restoring Force

    A restoring force is a force that always acts toward the equilibrium position. It is responsible for bringing the system back whenever it is displaced.

    Without a restoring force, oscillatory motion cannot occur.

    Simple Harmonic Motion

    Simple harmonic motion is a special type of oscillatory motion in which the restoring force is directly proportional to displacement and opposite in direction.

    This proportionality leads to sinusoidal motion and makes SHM mathematically simple yet physically powerful.

    Spring–Mass System

    A mass attached to a spring exhibits simple harmonic motion when displaced from equilibrium and released.

    The stiffness of the spring determines how quickly the system oscillates.

    Simple Pendulum

    A simple pendulum consists of a mass suspended by a light string. For small angular displacements, its motion closely approximates SHM.

    The period of a pendulum depends on its length and the local gravitational field.

    Energy in Oscillatory Motion

    During oscillations, energy continuously transforms between kinetic and potential forms.

    In ideal systems without losses, total mechanical energy remains constant.

    Damped Oscillations

    Real oscillatory systems lose energy due to friction, air resistance, or internal forces.

    As a result, the amplitude of oscillations gradually decreases with time.

    Forced Oscillations

    Forced oscillations occur when an external periodic force continuously supplies energy to a system.

    The response of the system depends on the frequency of the applied force.

    Resonance

    Resonance occurs when the frequency of the external force matches the natural frequency of the system.

    At resonance, energy transfer is most efficient, leading to large oscillation amplit.
