    Electromagnetic Waves

    Electromagnetic waves are self-sustaining oscillations of electric and magnetic fields. They require no medium and travel at the speed of light.

    Core Ideas

    Nature of Electromagnetic Waves

    EM waves consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to each other.

    They propagate without requiring material medium.

    Maxwell’s Prediction

    Maxwell showed that changing electric and magnetic fields sustain each other.

    This led to prediction of electromagnetic waves.

    Speed of Electromagnetic Waves

    EM waves travel at speed c in vacuum.

    This connected light with electromagnetism.

    Electromagnetic Spectrum

    The spectrum ranges from radio waves to gamma rays.

    Different frequencies have different applications.

    Energy Transport

    EM waves carry energy through space.

    This energy can be absorbed by matter.

    Momentum of EM Waves

    EM waves carry momentum despite having no mass.

    This leads to radiation pressure.

    Applications

    EM waves enable communication, medical imaging, astronomy, and modern technology.

    They are essential to modern civilization.
