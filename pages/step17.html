    Thermal Physics

    Thermal physics studies heat, temperature, and their relationship to energy. It connects microscopic motion of particles to macroscopic physical behavior.

    Core Ideas

    Temperature

    Temperature measures the average kinetic energy of particles in a system.

    It determines the direction of heat flow between bodies.

    Heat

    Heat is energy transferred due to a temperature difference.

    It is not a substance, but a mode of energy transfer.

    Thermal Equilibrium

    Two systems are in thermal equilibrium when no net heat flows between them.

    This concept underlies the definition of temperature.

    Thermal Expansion

    Most materials expand when heated due to increased particle motion.

    Expansion must be considered in engineering and construction.

    Specific Heat Capacity

    Specific heat capacity measures how much heat is required to change a substance’s temperature.

    Modes of Heat Transfer

    Heat is transferred via conduction, convection, and radiation.

    Each mode operates through different physical mechanisms.
