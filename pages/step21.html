    Electric Potential

    Electric potential provides an energy-based way to understand electric fields. It simplifies complex force calculations and plays a key role in circuit theory.

    Core Ideas

    Electric Potential Energy

    Electric potential energy arises due to the position of a charge in an electric field. Work is done when a charge moves under electric forces.

    This energy depends on charge magnitude and field configuration.

    Electric Potential

    Electric potential is defined as potential energy per unit charge.

    It allows comparison of energy changes without specifying the test charge.

    Equipotential Surfaces

    Equipotential surfaces are regions where electric potential is constant.

    No work is done when a charge moves along an equipotential surface.

    Relation Between Electric Field and Potential

    The electric field is the negative gradient of electric potential.

    Strong fields correspond to rapidly changing potential.

    Capacitors

    Capacitors store electric charge and energy using electric fields.

    They are fundamental components in electronic circuits.

    Energy Stored in Capacitors

    Energy stored in a capacitor resides in the electric field between its plates.

    This stored energy can be released when the circuit is completed.
