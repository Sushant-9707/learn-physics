    Fluid Mechanics

    Fluid mechanics studies the behavior of liquids and gases at rest and in motion. Despite their ability to flow, fluids obey precise physical laws that explain phenomena ranging from blood circulation to atmospheric motion.

    Core Ideas

    What is a Fluid?

    A fluid is a substance that continuously deforms under applied stress. Liquids and gases are both fluids, differing mainly in compressibility.

    Fluids do not resist shear stress in the way solids do.

    Pressure in Fluids

    Pressure is the force exerted per unit area by a fluid. In fluids at rest, pressure acts equally in all directions.

    Pressure increases with depth due to the weight of the fluid above.

    Buoyancy & Archimedes’ Principle

    An object immersed in a fluid experiences an upward force called buoyant force.

    This force equals the weight of the fluid displaced by the object.

    Fluid Statics

    Fluid statics deals with fluids at rest. It explains pressure variation, hydraulic systems, and forces on submerged surfaces.

    Fluid Dynamics

    Fluid dynamics studies fluids in motion. It focuses on flow speed, streamlines, and energy conservation.

    Equation of Continuity

    The continuity equation expresses conservation of mass in fluid flow.

    When flow speed increases, cross-sectional area decreases, and vice versa.

    Bernoulli’s Principle

    Bernoulli’s principle relates pressure, velocity, and height in flowing fluids.

    Faster-moving fluids exert lower pressure.

    Viscosity

    Viscosity measures a fluid’s resistance to flow. It represents internal friction between fluid layers.
