STEP 06

Applications of Newton’s Laws

Newton’s laws become truly useful when applied to real systems. This section focuses on analyzing forces in everyday situations using free-body diagrams and careful force balance.

Core Ideas

Free Body Diagrams

A free body diagram represents all external forces acting on a single object. It is the most important tool for applying Newton’s laws correctly.

Each force is drawn as a vector, allowing equations of motion to be written along chosen axes.

Friction

Friction is a force that opposes relative motion between surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surface and opposite to the direction of motion.

Static friction prevents motion, while kinetic friction acts during motion.

Tension in Strings

Tension is the force transmitted through a string, rope, or cable when pulled by forces acting at either end.

In ideal strings, tension is uniform throughout and always acts along the string.

Inclined Planes

An inclined plane allows motion to be analyzed by resolving forces parallel and perpendicular to the surface.

Gravity is split into components, simplifying the application of Newton’s laws.

Pulley Systems

Pulley systems involve multiple connected bodies and strings, often requiring simultaneous equations to solve.

They are excellent examples of Newton’s third law and constraint-based motion.
