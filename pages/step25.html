    Alternating Current

    Alternating current reverses direction periodically. It is used for power transmission because it can be efficiently transformed and transmitted.

    Core Ideas

    Nature of Alternating Current

    Alternating current changes magnitude and direction with time, usually sinusoidally.

    This periodic behavior allows efficient energy transfer.

    AC vs DC

    DC flows in one direction, while AC reverses direction.

    AC is preferred for large-scale power distribution.

    RMS Values

    RMS value represents the effective value of AC, comparable to DC heating effect.

    Household voltage ratings are RMS values.

    AC Circuits

    AC circuits may contain resistors, capacitors, and inductors.

    Phase differences arise between voltage and current.

    Reactance

    Reactance is opposition to AC due to capacitors and inductors.

    It depends on frequency of the alternating current.

    Impedance

    Impedance combines resistance and reactance into a single quantity.

    It determines current magnitude in AC circuits.

    Power in AC Circuits

    Power in AC depends on phase difference between voltage and current.

    Power factor measures efficiency of power usage.
