    Laws of Thermodynamics

    The laws of thermodynamics govern energy, heat, and entropy. They place fundamental limits on what processes can occur in nature, independent of specific materials or systems.

    Core Ideas

    Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

    If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.

    This law provides the basis for temperature measurement.

    First Law of Thermodynamics

    The first law expresses conservation of energy. It relates changes in internal energy to heat added and work done.

    Second Law of Thermodynamics

    The second law introduces irreversibility in natural processes.

    Heat flows spontaneously from hot to cold, not the reverse.

    Entropy

    Entropy measures the degree of disorder or randomness in a system.

    In isolated systems, entropy tends to increase over time.

    Heat Engines

    Heat engines convert thermal energy into mechanical work.

    Their efficiency is fundamentally limited by the laws of thermodynamics.

    Refrigerators & Heat Pumps

    These devices transfer heat against its natural direction using external work.

    Third Law of Thermodynamics

    As temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero.
